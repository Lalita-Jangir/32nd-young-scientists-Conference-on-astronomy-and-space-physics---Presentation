Abstract 

In recent studies of spinning particles in black hole (BH) phenomenology, it has been shown that spinning particles provide theoretical coherence when treated as effective test probes of strong gravitational fields with quantum/matter-induced corrections in spacetime. Motivated by such studies, we chose to analyse the dynamics of a neutral spinning test particle revolving around a short-hair BH, which circumvents the “no-short-hair” theorem. The strength of short hair BH is characterised by the parameter Qm, and due to its impact near the outer horizon, it may provide inside information about the BH. The importance of this spacetime also lies in providing a mathematical laboratory for quantum-gravity effects. In order to explore the motion of spinning particles in the equatorial plane around this manifold, we have used the Mathisson-Papapetrou-Dixon (MPD) equations along with the Tulczyjew supplementary condition, and investigated two important classes of circular orbits, the marginally bound orbit (MBO) and the innermost stable orbit (ISCO), alongside periodic orbits. This analysis isolates genuine short-hair effects on the trajectories of spinning particles and can provide leverage for the potential observational signatures. The present work is significant because astrophysical particles have spins and do not follow standard geodesics due to the interaction between their intrinsic angular momentum and the Riemann curvature tensor, leading to divergent behaviour.
